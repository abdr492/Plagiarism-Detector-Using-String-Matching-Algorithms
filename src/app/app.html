Advanced DSA Research Lab

Classic String-Matching Meet Paraphrase NLP

Welcome to PlagDetect. Traditional engines struggle to spot smart edits, and AI summaries struggle to pinpoint exact word coordinates. Our pipeline integrates exact linear-time trackers (KMP, Rabin-Karp) with structural local hash minima fingerprinting (Winnowing) and cosine distribution metrics to capture plagiarism—even if words are shuffled or spaces omitted.

Active Matching Pipelines

font_download
O(N + M)

Knuth-Morris-Pratt (KMP)

Avoids redundancy by pre-computing a state machine of the pattern (LPS table). Skips checked alignments upon failure. Ideal of exact continuous phrases search.

toll
O(N + M) avg

Rabin-Karp Rolling Hashing

Encodes text substrings into modulo integer rolling hashes. Extremely fast for scanning multiple patterns concurrently since shifting hash handles constant $O(1)$ time.

view_comfy
O(N)

MOSS Fingerprinting

Sanitizes words, generates hashed character k-grams, and picks mathematical local minima in sliding windows. Identifies matches even with added spaces or adjectives.

extension
O(N)

Jaccard Shingle Coef

Breaks comparison documents into continuous word shingles (3-grams) and measures set intersections divided by their union. Gives the general composition resemblance.

bubble_chart
O(V)

Cosine Vector Spaces

Maps vocabulary word-frequency counts as multi-dimensional vectors and measures the cosine angle. Flags documents dealing with identical topics even with swapped synonyms.

insights
O(N * M)

Levenshtein & LCS

Levenshtein measures raw word editing distance ($O(NM)$ DP matrix), while word LCS backtracks key continuous patterns to identify paraphrased sections line-by-line.
